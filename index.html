Bio

I am an incoming PhD student at the University of Florida, working with Dr. Xilei Zhao in the SERMoS Lab at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Transportation Engineering. My current research focuses on developing interpretable machine learning models with fairness constraints for transportation systems, including a unified framework for addressing fairness across the entire travel modeling pipeline (trip generation, destination distribution, mode choice, and traffic assignment). I am also exploring bi-directional fairness frameworks to prevent biases from propagating between modeling stages.

I recently graduated from Colorado College with a double major in Computer Science and Mathematics, where I developed FORMAL—an AI system that integrates Retrieval-Augmented Thinking and agentic feedback loops for translating informal mathematics into Lean 4 code with 92% syntactic correctness.

My research interests include LLM Agents, Trustworthy AI, and AI for Transportation. I'm particularly excited about:

  • Designing interpretable and fair AI models for transportation demand forecasting that balance predictive accuracy with equity considerations
  • Developing integrated approaches to ensure fairness across multi-stage travel modeling pipelines
  • Creating real-time digital twins powered by LLM-based agents for adaptive transportation systems monitoring and management
  • Building robust validation frameworks for agent-based transportation models with resource-efficient architectures
